SoFi Stadium — your 2026 World Cup fan guide

Hosts 8 matches. Inglewood, LA. Newest US WC venue, opened 2020.

SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California, hosts 8 matches at the 2026 World Cup including knockout rounds. Capacity: 70,000 (expandable to 100,000). The newest US WC venue and the most expensive stadium ever built ($5.5B). Here's everything you need.

How to get there

By Metro (LA Metro K Line)

The K Line opened in 2022 and reaches SoFi Stadium directly. From downtown LA, take the K Line to Downtown Inglewood Station, then 10-minute shuttle to stadium. 45 minutes total. Fare: $1.75.

From LAX (Los Angeles International Airport)

LAX is 4 miles from SoFi. Options:
• Free LAX-to-Metro shuttle to K Line, then K Line to SoFi (~30 mins)
• Uber/Lyft direct: 15 mins, $15-30
• Driving: 10-25 mins depending on traffic

From downtown LA

K Line: 45 mins, $1.75
Driving: 30-60 mins, $40+ for stadium parking

Parking

Stadium parking is $50+/day. Better to use rideshare (Uber/Lyft pickup areas are well-marked) or LA Metro.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get to SoFi Stadium without a car?

LA Metro K Line directly. From downtown LA, take K Line to Downtown Inglewood Station then short shuttle. 45 minutes total, $1.75.

How far is SoFi from LAX?

4 miles. By LAX-Metro shuttle + K Line: 30 mins. By Uber: 15 mins, $15-30. By car: 10-25 mins depending on traffic.

Where should I stay for SoFi Stadium matches?

Inglewood / LAX area is closest and easiest. Santa Monica is more scenic. Downtown LA is more cultural. Each has tradeoffs in price and vibe.

Why is SoFi Stadium famous?

It opened in 2020 and is the most expensive stadium ever built at $5.5 billion. Features a translucent ETFE roof, 70,000 seats expandable to 100,000, and is home to both LA Rams and LA Chargers.
