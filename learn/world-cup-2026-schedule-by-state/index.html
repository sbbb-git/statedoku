Travel strategy by region

East Coast cluster

NY/NJ + Boston + Philadelphia + Miami + Atlanta, covering 36 matches. Best base: NYC area for the early rounds, then move south. East Coast venues are within 1-3 hour flights of each other.

Texas hub

Dallas + Houston host 16 matches, the highest of any state. 3.5-hour drive or 1-hour flight between them. Great base for fans following teams in groups stationed centrally.

West Coast cluster

LA + SF Bay + Seattle, 20 matches. Long distances between (6 hours LA-Seattle by car), but cheap one-way flights. Best for fans who want to chase specific teams.

Frequently asked questions

Which US state hosts the most World Cup matches?

Texas, with 16 matches across Dallas (9) and Houston (7). California is second with 14 (LA + SF Bay).

Which state hosts the Final?

New Jersey hosts the Final at MetLife Stadium on July 19, 2026, even though it's called the "New York / New Jersey" host venue.

How many matches are in the US total?

78 of the 104 tournament matches will be played in the USA. The other 26 are in Mexico (13) and Canada (13).

Which states are NOT hosting any World Cup matches?

41 US states are not hosting matches: Alabama, Alaska, Arizona, Arkansas, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Montana, Nebraska, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Mexico, New York (matches are in NJ), North Carolina, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, Rhode Island, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, West Virginia, Wisconsin, Wyoming.
