How the second US-hosted World Cup compares to the first. Teams, matches, venues, revenue.

What 1994 changed forever

USA 1994 transformed the world game:

  • Highest attendance per match ever: 68,991 average (record stood until 2026)
  • FIFA's first $1B+ revenue WC
  • 3 points for a win debuted (replacing 2)
  • Major League Soccer (MLS) born as part of US WC bid commitments — first MLS season 1996
  • Final stadium not soccer-purpose-built — Rose Bowl was historic for football fans but built for college football

What 2026 changes again

2026 will be the first to:

  • Host 48 teams in a Round of 32 format
  • Be co-hosted by 3 nations
  • Have 3 mascots (Maple, Zayu, Clutch)
  • Span 39 days (Qatar 2022 was 28)
  • Use Goal-Line Technology + VAR universally
  • Generate $11B+ in revenue (Qatar was $7.5B)

Iconic moments from 1994 to remember

  • Brazil 0-0 Italy — first WC Final decided on penalties (Brazil won 3-2)
  • Baggio's miss — Roberto Baggio sent his penalty over the bar
  • Bulgaria's run — went all the way to the semifinals as huge underdogs
  • Andrés Escobar tragedy — Colombian defender killed days after scoring an own goal vs USA
  • Maradona's exit — Argentina's Maradona tested positive for ephedrine, sent home
  • Stoichkov's coronation — Bulgarian striker won Golden Boot

Frequently asked questions

When was the previous US World Cup?

1994. The US hosted the entire tournament. Brazil won by beating Italy on penalties in the Rose Bowl Final.

How many more matches in 2026 than 1994?

52 more (104 vs 52). The expansion to 48 teams + the new Round of 32 stage explain the increase.

Where was the 1994 World Cup Final?

Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. Brazil beat Italy 3-2 on penalties after 0-0.

How many US venues in 1994 vs 2026?

9 in 1994 (Boston, Chicago, Dallas, Detroit, LA, NYC, Orlando, San Francisco, Washington DC). 11 in 2026 (Atlanta, Boston, Dallas, Houston, Kansas City, LA, Miami, NY/NJ, Philadelphia, SF Bay, Seattle).
