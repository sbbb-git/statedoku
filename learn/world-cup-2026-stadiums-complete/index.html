Largest stadium of the tournament

Estadio Azteca in Mexico City is the largest WC 2026 venue with ~87,000 capacity. It will be the first stadium ever to host 3 different World Cups (1970, 1986, 2026).

Newest WC stadium

SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles (opened September 2020). Cost: $5.5 billion — the most expensive stadium ever built. Translucent ETFE roof.

Oldest WC stadium

Estadio Azteca, opened in 1966. Site of Pelé's "Game of the Century" (1970), Maradona's "Hand of God" + "Goal of the Century" (1986).

Stadium for the Final

MetLife Stadium (New Jersey) hosts the Final on July 19, 2026. 82,500 seats. Read more at The Final at MetLife →

Frequently asked questions

How many stadiums host the 2026 World Cup?

16 stadiums across 3 countries: 11 in the USA, 2 in Canada (Toronto, Vancouver), and 3 in Mexico (Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey).

What's the largest 2026 World Cup stadium?

Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, with ~87,000 capacity. It's also the first stadium ever to host 3 World Cups (1970, 1986, 2026).

What's the newest stadium?

SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, opened September 2020. Cost $5.5 billion — most expensive stadium ever built.

What stadium hosts the Final?

MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey. 82,500 seats. July 19, 2026.
