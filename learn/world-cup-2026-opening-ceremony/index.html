World Cup 2026 — opening ceremony

June 11, 2026. Estadio Azteca, Mexico City. What to expect before the first whistle.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup opening ceremony takes place on June 11, 2026 at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, immediately preceding the opening match. It's the global kickoff to 39 days of football.

Date
June 11, 2026
Venue
Estadio Azteca, Mexico City
Capacity
~87,000
Format
~30 minutes preceding the opener

What to expect

FIFA opening ceremonies traditionally include:

  • A blend of host country's cultural performances (Mexican mariachi, regional music)
  • A massive stadium-wide light show / pyrotechnics
  • One or two global pop stars performing the official anthem and tournament song
  • A formal speech from FIFA's president (Gianni Infantino)
  • The hoisting of all 48 team flags
  • The tournament's "trophy procession" — the FIFA World Cup trophy displayed

Past opening ceremony highlights

  • 2022 Qatar: Morgan Freeman + Trinidad Cardona. Memorable for cultural mix.
  • 2018 Russia: Robbie Williams. Iconic moment with his children.
  • 2014 Brazil: Pitbull, Jennifer Lopez, Claudia Leitte performing "We Are One".
  • 2010 South Africa: Shakira leading "Waka Waka".
  • 1994 USA: Diana Ross missed the goal in her stunt!

Who will perform in 2026?

Performers are typically announced 3-6 months before the tournament. Rumored options for 2026 (NOT confirmed):

  • A Mexican mainstream artist (Bad Bunny, Maluma)
  • An American pop icon (Shakira, BTS member)
  • A Canadian artist (Drake, Justin Bieber, The Weeknd)

FIFA confirms the lineup officially via fifa.com/worldcup.

The 2026 official anthem

FIFA traditionally announces the official anthem 6-12 months before the tournament. The 2026 song was unveiled in 2025 and features artists from all 3 host countries.

How to watch the ceremony

The ceremony airs on:

  • USA: Fox Sports + Telemundo (Spanish)
  • Mexico: Televisa + Univisión
  • UK: BBC + ITV
  • Worldwide: FIFA+ streaming app
