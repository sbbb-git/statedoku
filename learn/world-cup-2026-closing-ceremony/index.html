World Cup 2026 — closing ceremony

July 19, 2026. MetLife Stadium, New Jersey. Before the Final.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup closing ceremony takes place on July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, immediately preceding the Final. The Final caps 39 days of competition.

What's the closing ceremony?

Unlike the opening ceremony, the closing ceremony is more compact — typically 15-20 minutes. It serves as a transition between the 3rd place match (July 18) and the Final, marking the end of the tournament. Format:

  • A musical performance (1-2 acts)
  • A pyrotechnics/lights display
  • Brief tribute to the host nations
  • Optional player tributes or hall-of-fame moments

Past closing ceremony highlights

  • 2022 Qatar: Combined with opening — focus on cultural displays.
  • 2018 Russia: Tchaikovsky, Russian ballet.
  • 2014 Brazil: Shakira + Wyclef Jean — "La La La" remix.
  • 2010 South Africa: Shakira + Spanish artists.

The MetLife venue

MetLife Stadium seats 82,500 — the largest US venue. It's hosting a Super Bowl in 2014, the WrestleMania in 2013, and now the WC Final. The ceremony will be designed for both stadium spectators and global TV audience (1B+).

The award ceremony (post-final)

After the Final whistle, the formal award ceremony begins:

  • Bronze medal: 3rd place team
  • Silver medal: Final losers
  • Trophy presentation: Winners receive the FIFA World Cup trophy from FIFA's president + host country leader
  • Golden Boot: Top scorer of the tournament
  • Golden Ball: Best player of the tournament (Adidas)
  • Golden Glove: Best goalkeeper
  • Best Young Player: Under 21

The 2022 trophy lift

Messi's iconic trophy lift in 2022 Qatar is the benchmark moment. 2026 winner will mark either Mbappé's 2nd or a new era.

Frequently asked questions

When is the closing ceremony?

July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium, New Jersey, immediately before the Final.

How long does the closing ceremony last?

Approximately 15-20 minutes, before the Final kickoff.

Who performs at the closing ceremony?

Performers are confirmed 1-2 months before the Final. Past ceremonies featured Shakira, Wyclef Jean, and major global pop stars.

What awards are given after the Final?

FIFA awards the World Cup trophy, Golden Boot (top scorer), Golden Ball (best player), Golden Glove (best goalkeeper), and Best Young Player.
