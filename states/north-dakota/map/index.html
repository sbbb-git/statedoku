North Dakota on the US map

North Dakota is located in the Midwest region of the United States — specifically the plains subregion. With an area ranking of #19 of 50, it is a moderately-sized state. The map above highlights North Dakota in gold and color-codes the other 49 states by region.

Key cities

The capital of North Dakota is Bismarck. The largest city by population is Fargo. As is the case with most US states, the capital is not the largest city — this is by design, dating back to the late 18th and 19th centuries when state founders deliberately chose smaller, more centrally located towns to host state government and avoid concentrating political and commercial power in the same place.

Borders and neighbors

North Dakota shares land borders with 3 other US states: Montana, South Dakota, Minnesota.

North Dakota also shares an international border with Canada — part of the longest international border in the world.

Geographic features at a glance

  • Population: under 1 million
  • Coastline: landlocked (no ocean access)

Common questions

Where is North Dakota located?

North Dakota is in the Midwest region of the United States. Its capital is Bismarck and its largest city is Fargo. The state ranks #19 of 50 by total area.

What states border North Dakota?

North Dakota borders Montana, South Dakota, Minnesota. That's 3 bordering states in total.

Is North Dakota on the coast?

No, North Dakota is landlocked. It has no ocean coastline and no Great Lakes shoreline.

What time zone is North Dakota in?

North Dakota observes Central Time (the state spans multiple time zones).
