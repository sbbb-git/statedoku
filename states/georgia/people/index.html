Notable natives of Georgia

Over its 238-year history as a state, Georgia has produced notable figures across politics, business, sports, entertainment, science, military service, and the arts. Some are world-famous; others are remembered mainly by Georgia residents. This page covers categories to give you a sense of the state's cultural and historical contributions.

US presidents from Georgia

Georgia is one of the 21 US states that has produced at least one US president. Virginia leads with 8 presidents, Ohio is second with 7. The state is part of an exclusive group whose native sons (and one day, hopefully, daughters) have reached the White House. See the Wikipedia page "List of US presidential birthplaces" for the comprehensive list.

Politicians

Georgia has been represented in the US Senate (2 seats) and US House (proportional to population) throughout its history. The state has also produced governors, mayors, US Supreme Court justices, ambassadors, and cabinet members. State politics typically reflects the broader political lean: competitive between parties (swing state).

Entertainment industry

Georgia has contributed actors, musicians, comedians, directors, and producers to Hollywood, Broadway, and the music industry. The state is itself a major film-production destination. Country music has significant ties to the state.

Athletes

Georgia has produced Hall of Fame athletes across major US sports (baseball, football, basketball, hockey, soccer, golf, tennis, boxing, NASCAR, etc.) as well as Olympic medalists. Pro franchises in the state include teams across NFL, NBA, MLB.

Business and tech

Georgia has produced founders, CEOs, inventors, and investors across many industries. Agricultural business leaders. For tech specifically, California, Washington, Massachusetts, and New York dominate, but every state has contributed to American business history.

Science and academia

Georgia hosts research universities, science museums, and (in some cases) federal research facilities. Nobel laureates and Pulitzer winners have come from the state — see the Wikipedia "List of Nobel laureates by country" for the breakdown by US state.

Military and civic service

Georgia residents have served in every American war from the Revolution to today. Veterans, civil rights leaders, and community organizers from Georgia have shaped both the state and the nation.

Where to find a comprehensive list

For the most thorough, up-to-date list of notable people from Georgia, see Wikipedia's "List of people from Georgia" or the state historical society's records. We deliberately don't try to list every notable person here — there are simply too many, and the list changes as new figures emerge.
