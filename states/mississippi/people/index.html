Notable natives of Mississippi

Over its 209-year history as a state (and longer as a territory before that), Mississippi has produced notable figures across virtually every field of American life: politics, business, sports, entertainment, science, military service, civil rights, and the arts. Some are world-famous; others are remembered mainly by state residents. This page covers categories to give you a sense of the state's cultural and historical contributions.

Politicians and public servants

Mississippi has been represented in the US Senate (2 seats since statehood) and US House (proportional to population) throughout its history. The state has also produced governors, mayors, US Supreme Court justices, ambassadors, cabinet members, and civil rights leaders. State politics historically reflects the broader political lean: Republican-leaning in recent decades, though local politics can differ from the statewide trend.

Entertainment industry

Mississippi has contributed actors, musicians, comedians, directors, and producers to Hollywood, Broadway, and the music industry. Blues music originated or thrived here. For comprehensive lists by category (actors, musicians, comedians, directors), see Wikipedia's lists of people from Mississippi by profession.

Athletes — Hall of Famers and Olympians

Mississippi has produced Hall of Fame athletes across major US sports (baseball, football, basketball, hockey, soccer, golf, tennis, boxing, NASCAR, etc.) as well as Olympic medalists across summer and winter games. The state has no major pro franchises but produces athletes who compete elsewhere. College athletics remain hugely important: many future pros begin their careers at state universities or smaller schools within Mississippi.

Business and tech entrepreneurs

Mississippi has produced founders, CEOs, inventors, and investors across many industries. Agricultural business leaders. For tech specifically, California, Washington, Massachusetts, and New York dominate, but every state has contributed to American business history. Many Fortune 500 CEOs grew up in or studied in Mississippi.

Scientists and academics

Mississippi hosts research universities, science museums, and (in some cases) federal research facilities. Nobel laureates and Pulitzer winners have come from the state — see the Wikipedia "List of Nobel laureates by country" for the breakdown by US state, and search Pulitzer's website for state-specific Pulitzer Prize winners.

Writers, journalists, and intellectuals

Mississippi has produced novelists, poets, journalists, and historians who have shaped American letters. The state's libraries, literary magazines, and writing programs (particularly at major universities) have nurtured generations of writers.

Civil rights and social movements

The Civil Rights Movement of the mid-20th century included leaders, activists, and martyrs from Mississippi. Earlier movements — abolition, women's suffrage, labor — also had Mississippi chapters. Subsequent movements for racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+ rights, immigration reform, and economic justice have continued to include Mississippi voices.

Military service

Mississippi residents have served in every American war from the Revolution to today. Medal of Honor recipients, generals, admirals, and decorated combat veterans from Mississippi have shaped both the state and the nation.

Where to find a comprehensive list

For the most thorough, up-to-date list of notable people from Mississippi, see Wikipedia's "List of people from Mississippi" or the state historical society's records. The state's tourism website often has a "Famous people" or "Notable natives" page. Local newspapers and university libraries are also excellent resources.

🌟 Mississippi trivia

  • The state is home to the birthplace of Elvis Presley (Tupelo) and the blues (Delta region).
  • It's one of the only states with a name containing four "i"s, four "s"s, and two "p"s — a spelling-bee favorite.
  • Mississippi has the most miles of navigable inland waterways of any state.
  • Root beer was invented in Biloxi by pharmacist Edward Adolf Barq Sr. in 1898.

Frequently asked questions about people from Mississippi

How many presidents were born in Mississippi?

Mississippi has not been the birthplace of a US president.

Who are famous people from Mississippi?

Mississippi has produced notable figures across politics, sports, entertainment, business, science, and the arts. See Wikipedia's "List of people from Mississippi" for the most comprehensive list.

Are there famous musicians from Mississippi?

Yes — Mississippi has produced notable musicians. The state has a strong blues tradition.

Are there famous actors from Mississippi?

Yes, Mississippi has contributed to film and television since both industries began.

Are there Hall of Fame athletes from Mississippi?

Yes — Mississippi has produced Hall of Fame athletes across all major American sports leagues.

Are there Nobel laureates from Mississippi?

Several. The state's research universities have produced or hosted Nobel Prize winners in various fields.

Have any US Supreme Court justices been from Mississippi?

Multiple Supreme Court justices have come from Mississippi throughout US history. See the Supreme Court's website for the official list.

Has Mississippi produced presidential candidates who didn't win?

Mississippi has produced major-party presidential nominees who lost their elections. The history is dotted with such figures.
