Notable natives of Delaware

Over its 239-year history as a state (and longer as a territory before that), Delaware has produced notable figures across virtually every field of American life: politics, business, sports, entertainment, science, military service, civil rights, and the arts. Some are world-famous; others are remembered mainly by state residents. This page covers categories to give you a sense of the state's cultural and historical contributions.

US presidents from Delaware

Delaware is one of the 21 US states that has produced at least one US president. The "president birth state" leaderboard is dominated by Virginia (8 presidents — Washington, Jefferson, Madison, Monroe, William Henry Harrison, Tyler, Taylor, Wilson) and Ohio (7 — Grant, Hayes, Garfield, Benjamin Harrison, McKinley, Taft, Harding), with several states tied for third. See the Wikipedia page "List of US presidential birthplaces" for the comprehensive list and details on each president's connection to Delaware.

Politicians and public servants

Delaware has been represented in the US Senate (2 seats since statehood) and US House (proportional to population) throughout its history. The state has also produced governors, mayors, US Supreme Court justices, ambassadors, cabinet members, and civil rights leaders. State politics historically reflects the broader political lean: Democratic-leaning in recent decades, though local politics can differ from the statewide trend.

Entertainment industry

Delaware has contributed actors, musicians, comedians, directors, and producers to Hollywood, Broadway, and the music industry. For comprehensive lists by category (actors, musicians, comedians, directors), see Wikipedia's lists of people from Delaware by profession.

Athletes — Hall of Famers and Olympians

Delaware has produced Hall of Fame athletes across major US sports (baseball, football, basketball, hockey, soccer, golf, tennis, boxing, NASCAR, etc.) as well as Olympic medalists across summer and winter games. The state has no major pro franchises but produces athletes who compete elsewhere. College athletics remain hugely important: many future pros begin their careers at state universities or smaller schools within Delaware.

Business and tech entrepreneurs

Delaware has produced founders, CEOs, inventors, and investors across many industries. For tech specifically, California, Washington, Massachusetts, and New York dominate, but every state has contributed to American business history. Many Fortune 500 CEOs grew up in or studied in Delaware.

Scientists and academics

Delaware hosts research universities, science museums, and (in some cases) federal research facilities. Nobel laureates and Pulitzer winners have come from the state — see the Wikipedia "List of Nobel laureates by country" for the breakdown by US state, and search Pulitzer's website for state-specific Pulitzer Prize winners.

Writers, journalists, and intellectuals

Delaware has produced novelists, poets, journalists, and historians who have shaped American letters. The state's libraries, literary magazines, and writing programs (particularly at major universities) have nurtured generations of writers.

Civil rights and social movements

The Civil Rights Movement of the mid-20th century included leaders, activists, and martyrs from Delaware. Earlier movements — abolition, women's suffrage, labor — also had Delaware chapters. Subsequent movements for racial justice, gender equity, LGBTQ+ rights, immigration reform, and economic justice have continued to include Delaware voices.

Military service

Delaware residents have served in every American war from the Revolution to today. Medal of Honor recipients, generals, admirals, and decorated combat veterans from Delaware have shaped both the state and the nation.

Where to find a comprehensive list

For the most thorough, up-to-date list of notable people from Delaware, see Wikipedia's "List of people from Delaware" or the state historical society's records. The state's tourism website often has a "Famous people" or "Notable natives" page. Local newspapers and university libraries are also excellent resources.

Delaware notable firsts

  • First state to ratify the US Constitution (December 7, 1787)

🌟 Delaware trivia

  • Was the first state to ratify the US Constitution, on December 7, 1787.
  • It's the second smallest state by area but home to more than half of all Fortune 500 companies (legally — they incorporate here for tax reasons).
  • The state has only three counties — the fewest of any state.
  • President Joe Biden was born in Scranton, PA, but represented Delaware in the US Senate for 36 years.

Frequently asked questions about people from Delaware

How many presidents were born in Delaware?

Delaware has produced at least one US president, joining a group of 21 US states with this distinction.

Who are famous people from Delaware?

Delaware has produced notable figures across politics, sports, entertainment, business, science, and the arts. See Wikipedia's "List of people from Delaware" for the most comprehensive list.

Are there famous musicians from Delaware?

Yes — Delaware has produced notable musicians.

Are there famous actors from Delaware?

Yes, Delaware has contributed to film and television since both industries began.

Are there Hall of Fame athletes from Delaware?

Yes — Delaware has produced Hall of Fame athletes across all major American sports leagues.

Are there Nobel laureates from Delaware?

Several. The state's research universities have produced or hosted Nobel Prize winners in various fields.

Have any US Supreme Court justices been from Delaware?

Multiple Supreme Court justices have come from Delaware throughout US history. See the Supreme Court's website for the official list.

Has Delaware produced presidential candidates who didn't win?

Delaware has produced major-party presidential nominees who lost their elections. The history is dotted with such figures.
