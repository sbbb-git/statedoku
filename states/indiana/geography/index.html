Overview

Indiana occupies the 38th largest area among US states. Located in the Midwest region, specifically the midwest rust subregion, its physical geography reflects its position on the continent. It has coastline on the great_lakes.

Rivers and water

The state borders one or more of the Great Lakes — Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, or Ontario.

Mountains and elevation

Indiana has moderate elevation across most of its territory, without dominating mountain ranges.

Climate and time zone

Indiana falls in the Eastern time zone (the state spans multiple time zones — a small western or eastern portion may differ). Winters bring heavy snowfall, especially near the Great Lakes or in mountainous areas.

National parks and protected areas

The National Park Service manages many sites in Indiana including national parks, national monuments, national forests, and historic sites. Outdoor recreation is significant — hiking, camping, hunting, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Wildlife

Indiana's wildlife reflects its geography. Forests, plains, rivers, and (where applicable) coasts support a wide range of mammals, birds, fish, and reptiles. Protected populations of native species are maintained through state and federal wildlife management agencies.
