Texas on the US map

Texas is located in the South region of the United States — specifically the plains subregion. With an area ranking of #2 of 50, it is one of the largest US states. The map above highlights Texas in gold and color-codes the other 49 states by region.

Key cities

The capital of Texas is Austin. The largest city by population is Houston. As is the case with most US states, the capital is not the largest city — this is by design, dating back to the late 18th and 19th centuries when state founders deliberately chose smaller, more centrally located towns to host state government and avoid concentrating political and commercial power in the same place.

Borders and neighbors

Texas shares land borders with 4 other US states: New Mexico, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Louisiana.

Texas also shares an international border with Mexico — one of only 4 US states to do so (the others are California, Arizona, and New Mexico if not this state itself).

Geographic features at a glance

  • Total area rank: #2 of 50
  • Population: over 10 million
  • Coastline: gulf
  • Great Plains: yes — part of the central US plains

Common questions

Where is Texas located?

Texas is in the South region of the United States. Its capital is Austin and its largest city is Houston. The state ranks #2 of 50 by total area.

What states border Texas?

Texas borders New Mexico, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Louisiana. That's 4 bordering states in total.

Is Texas on the coast?

Yes, Texas has coastline on the gulf.

What time zone is Texas in?

Texas observes Central Time (the state spans multiple time zones).
