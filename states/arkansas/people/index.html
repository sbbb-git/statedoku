Notable natives of Arkansas

Over its 190-year history as a state, Arkansas has produced notable figures across politics, business, sports, entertainment, science, military service, and the arts. Some are world-famous; others are remembered mainly by Arkansas residents. This page covers categories to give you a sense of the state's cultural and historical contributions.

US presidents from Arkansas

Arkansas is one of the 21 US states that has produced at least one US president. Virginia leads with 8 presidents, Ohio is second with 7. The state is part of an exclusive group whose native sons (and one day, hopefully, daughters) have reached the White House. See the Wikipedia page "List of US presidential birthplaces" for the comprehensive list.

Politicians

Arkansas has been represented in the US Senate (2 seats) and US House (proportional to population) throughout its history. The state has also produced governors, mayors, US Supreme Court justices, ambassadors, and cabinet members. State politics typically reflects the broader political lean: Republican-leaning.

Entertainment industry

Arkansas has contributed actors, musicians, comedians, directors, and producers to Hollywood, Broadway, and the music industry.

Athletes

Arkansas has produced Hall of Fame athletes across major US sports (baseball, football, basketball, hockey, soccer, golf, tennis, boxing, NASCAR, etc.) as well as Olympic medalists. The state has no major pro franchises but produces athletes who compete elsewhere.

Business and tech

Arkansas has produced founders, CEOs, inventors, and investors across many industries. Agricultural business leaders. For tech specifically, California, Washington, Massachusetts, and New York dominate, but every state has contributed to American business history.

Science and academia

Arkansas hosts research universities, science museums, and (in some cases) federal research facilities. Nobel laureates and Pulitzer winners have come from the state — see the Wikipedia "List of Nobel laureates by country" for the breakdown by US state.

Military and civic service

Arkansas residents have served in every American war from the Revolution to today. Veterans, civil rights leaders, and community organizers from Arkansas have shaped both the state and the nation.

Where to find a comprehensive list
