### Description (paste into the long description editor, markdown OK)
```markdown
**Statedoku** is the daily puzzle game I wish existed: think Sudoku, but the cells are filled with US states instead of numbers, and each row and column has a real geographic constraint.

A typical 3×3 grid looks like this:

|  | Borders Mexico | Pacific Coast | Originally a Spanish colony |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Was admitted before 1900** | Texas | California | ... |
| **Has > 5M people** | ... | ... | ... |
| **Has no income tax** | ... | ... | ... |

You have 3 mistakes. A new puzzle drops every day at midnight ET.

### Why you might like it

- 🧠 **Real geographic logic**, not just memorization. You learn the country a little more each day.
- 🇺🇸 **All 50 states + DC** in play. Constraints range from political to physical to cultural.
- 🆓 **Free, no signup, no ads on launch.** Browser only.
- 🌍 **English, French, Spanish.**
- 📚 **21 supporting mini-games** if you want to drill specific skills (capitals, flags, abbreviations, silhouettes, electoral votes…).
- 🖨 **Printable worksheets** for teachers — every game has a classroom-ready PDF version.

### Where to play

The full experience (daily puzzle archive, leaderboard, themed mini-games, learn pages) lives at **[statedoku.com](https://statedoku.com)**. This itch page lets you sample the game right here in your browser.

### Credits

Built solo by [Sacha Bitoun](https://statedoku.com/about/). Geographic data from US Census, USGS, USPS, USDOT. Map graphics derived from public-domain government sources.

Feedback welcome — drop a comment below or email contact@statedoku.com.
```

1. Open the live page in incognito and play through one round to confirm the iframe loads correctly.
2. Pin the URL into a tweet from @Statedoku (the bot can ride this one):
   ```

   ```
3. Add the itch link to your `/press/` page on statedoku.com under "Where to play".
